Microbiome–Immune–Barrier Axis Integrator:

**Problem statement.** Tierney et al. (2024) and Park et al. (2024) both identify the same open question in their Discussions: spaceflight shifts the skin, oral, and gut microbiome and disrupts the skin barrier, but the causal links between (a) the Dragon capsule's environmental microbiome, (b) what colonizes the crew, (c) skin barrier breakdown, and (d) systemic immune and inflammatory response have not been integrated into a single cross-omic picture. The stool metagenomics dataset (OSD-630) in particular remains under-analyzed in the published literature.

**Approach.** Integrate seven of the twelve OSDR datasets — capsule swabs (OSD-573), crew skin/oral/nasal swabs (OSD-572), stool metagenomics (OSD-630), spatial skin transcriptomics (OSD-574), PBMC profiling (OSD-570), serum cytokines (OSD-575), and urine inflammation (OSD-656) — into a directed Sankey/graph map per astronaut. We compute (1) shared-taxa fraction between capsule surfaces and each crewmember's body sites, (2) correlations between site-specific microbial shifts and barrier-gene expression (FLG, CLDN, OCLN, HAS1/2/3), and (3) correlations between gut/oral microbial shifts and downstream cytokine and urine inflammation panels. Within-astronaut multi-site sampling provides the statistical structure; n=4 is honored by reporting per-astronaut effects with published Tierney/Park supplementary tables as priors rather than computing group-level p-values.

**Expected output.** A short astronaut-readable report plus a single signature visual: a per-crewmember "microbiome → barrier → immune" flow diagram showing which capsule taxa transferred to skin, which transfers correlated with barrier-gene downregulation, and which downstream cytokine or urine-inflammation markers tracked those shifts — with explicit uncertainty bands and a transparent methods note. Deliverable fits Track 1 (pathway-level perturbation across pre/in/post) and Track 2 (immune regulation and inflammation axes of the risk profile).
